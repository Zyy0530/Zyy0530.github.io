## Research Interests

<div class="research-summary">
  I am interested in how far emerging systems can push the automation of security research, especially work that still depends on careful manual validation. I stay open to new ideas, tools, and domains, and I enjoy learning by building. At the moment, my research centers on two closely connected directions:
</div>

<div class="research-area">
  <h3>Vulnerability Detection and Validation</h3>
  <p>I build LLM- and agent-based systems that can automatically discover vulnerabilities, explain why they happen, and validate them with concrete evidence such as executable proofs of concept.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>My recent work, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01317">TxRay</a>, automates root-cause analysis and PoC generation for DeFi incidents with strong accuracy and speed. Building on that prototype, Liyi and I developed <a href="https://x.com/clara_oracle">Clara</a> for real-time analysis of live blockchain attacks. Its public platform, <a href="https://www.clarahacks.com/">ClaraHacks</a>, now curates postmortems for roughly 400 incidents, each combining root-cause analysis with PoC generation.</li>
    <li>In my earlier work, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.21579">A2</a>, I identified and validated vulnerabilities in real Android applications, including a Google vulnerability that has since been fixed and another in <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=mark.via.gp">Via Browser</a>, an app with more than 10 million downloads, which has also been patched.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="research-area">
  <h3>AI Agents for Security Analysis</h3>
  <p>Beyond blockchain and Android applications, I am exploring how AI agents can support security analysis across a broader landscape. I am particularly interested in their use for program analysis, malware detection, and robotic or cyber-physical systems, where iterative reasoning and tool use can unlock new workflows for defenders and analysts.</p>
</div>
